**Persepolis: The Ancient Heart of Persia**

## متن

Persepolis, which is located near Shiraz, is one of the most impressive historical sites in Iran. This ancient city was founded by Darius the Great nearly 2,500 years ago. The ruins, which include tall columns and detailed stone carvings, show the advanced architecture of the Achaemenid Empire.  
Persepolis was a ceremonial capital, where kings held grand celebrations called “Nowruz.” Visitors who walk among these stones can imagine the glory of ancient Persia.  
Today, Persepolis attracts tourists from all over the world who want to experience the rich cultural heritage of Iran. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of the nation’s history.

**Naqsh-e Jahan Square: The Grand Heart of Isfahan**

## متن  

Naqsh-e Jahan Square, which is located in the center of Isfahan, is one of the largest historical squares in the world. It was built in the early 17th century during the Safavid dynasty. Around the square, there are important buildings such as the Shah Mosque, Sheikh Lotfollah Mosque, and Ali Qapu Palace, which all show the beauty of Persian-Islamic architecture.  

In the past, the square was a place where kings held ceremonies, watched polo games, and met foreign ambassadors. Today, it is a lively tourist attraction where visitors can walk, take photos, and enjoy traditional shops and tea houses. Naqsh-e Jahan is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that represents the cultural heritage of Isfahan and Iran.

Si-o-se-pol Bridge: The Iconic River Passage of Isfahan

### متن  
Si-o-se-pol, which is also known as the Bridge of 33 Arches, is one of the most famous bridges in Isfahan. It was built in the early 17th century during the Safavid dynasty and crosses the Zayandeh River. The bridge, which is made of brick and stone, is a beautiful example of Persian architecture and engineering.  

In the past, Si-o-se-pol was not only a bridge, but also a place where people gathered to walk, talk, and enjoy the view of the river. At night, the bridge created a magical atmosphere when its arches reflected in the water. Today, it is still a popular tourist attraction where locals and visitors sit, drink tea, and listen to the sound of the river.

## متن ۲ – Computer / تخصصی  
### عنوان متن  
Load Balancers: Distributing Traffic in Modern Web Systems

### متن  
In modern web applications, a load balancer is a crucial component which distributes incoming traffic across multiple servers. Instead of sending all requests to a single machine, the load balancer acts like a smart gateway that decides where each request should go. This helps prevent any server from becoming overloaded, which could slow down the system or even cause downtime.  

There are different types of load balancers, such as layer 4 and layer 7 load balancers, which make routing decisions based on various information. For example, a layer 7 load balancer can inspect the URL or headers and then direct the request to a specific service. In cloud environments, load balancing allows systems to scale horizontally, so new servers can be added when traffic increases and removed when it goes down.

Golestan Palace: The Royal Garden of Tehran

### متن  

Golestan Palace, which is located in the historic center of Tehran, is one of the oldest royal complexes in Iran. Most of its present buildings were constructed during the Qajar dynasty, which ruled the country from the late 18th to the early 20th century. The complex includes several halls, museums, and gardens that display a combination of traditional Persian art and European influences.  

Many important coronation ceremonies took place in Golestan Palace, where kings received foreign ambassadors and high-ranking officials. The mirrored halls and colorful tilework create a unique atmosphere that reflects the luxury of the Qajar court. Today, the palace is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist attraction that represents the cultural and political history of Tehran.

## متن ۲ – Computer / تخصصی  
### عنوان متن  
Microservices Architecture: Breaking Applications into Smaller Services

### متن  

Microservices architecture is a design approach in which a large application is built as a collection of small, independent services. Each service focuses on a specific business capability, such as user authentication, payment processing, or notification delivery. These services communicate with each other through lightweight protocols, often using HTTP or message queues.  

One of the main advantages of microservices is that teams can deploy and scale each service independently, which increases flexibility and resilience. If one service fails, it does not necessarily bring down the entire system. However, microservices also introduce challenges, such as more complex monitoring, distributed debugging, and the need for reliable service discovery and API gateways.

### متن  

Masuleh, which is located in the mountains of Gilan Province, is a village that is famous for its unique architecture. The houses are built on a steep hillside, so the roof of one house often becomes the yard of the house above it. Because of its foggy weather and traditional atmosphere, Masuleh feels like a place where time has slowed down. Many tourists visit the village to walk through the old bazaar, buy local handicrafts, and drink tea in small cafés that overlook the mountains.

## متن ۲ – Computer / تخصصی  
### عنوان  
**REST APIs: A Simple Way for Services to Talk**

### متن  

A REST API is an interface that allows different applications to communicate over HTTP. In this style, each resource, such as a user or an order, is represented by a URL, and clients send requests using standard methods like GET, POST, PUT, and DELETE. Because REST uses simple, stateless communication, it is easy to scale and debug.  

In modern systems, REST APIs act as a common language between frontend and backend services. For example, a web or mobile app can call an endpoint, which returns JSON data that the client can easily parse and display. Clear API design, which includes consistent naming and proper status codes, makes large systems easier to maintain and extend.

## متن ۲ – Computer  
### عنوان  
**Message Queues: Handling Tasks Asynchronously**

### متن  

A message queue is a component in distributed systems that allows services to communicate asynchronously. Instead of sending requests directly and waiting for a response, a producer sends messages to a queue, and one or more consumers process those messages later. This pattern, which decouples services, helps systems handle spikes in traffic and avoid overloading critical components.  

Message queues are widely used for background jobs, such as sending emails, processing images, or logging events. If one consumer fails, the messages usually remain in the queue until another worker is available. This improves reliability and makes it easier to scale specific parts of the system without changing the entire architecture.

## متن ۲ – Computer (.NET / Backend)  
### عنوان  
Dependency Injection: Keeping Your .NET Code Flexible  

### متن  

Dependency injection is a design pattern in which an object does not create its own dependencies but receives them from the outside. In .NET applications, developers often use a built-in container to register services and inject them where they are needed. This approach, which decouples components, makes the code easier to test, maintain, and extend.  

For example, instead of creating a new repository directly inside a controller, the controller receives an interface through its constructor. At runtime, the dependency injection container provides the correct implementation. This not only reduces tight coupling, but also allows developers to swap implementations, such as using a mock repository in unit tests or switching from a SQL database to another storage provider.

### عنوان  
**Middleware in ASP.NET Core: Processing Requests in a Pipeline**

### متن  

In ASP.NET Core, middleware components are arranged in a pipeline that processes every incoming HTTP request. Each middleware can inspect the request, perform some logic, and either handle the response or pass the request to the next component in the pipeline. This design, which follows the chain-of-responsibility pattern, makes it easy to add cross-cutting features such as logging, authentication, and error handling.  

Developers can use built-in middleware for common tasks, or define custom middleware for specific requirements. For example, a custom middleware might measure request duration, add custom headers, or redirect users under certain conditions. Because the order of middleware is important, careful configuration is required to ensure that each component runs at the right time.

### متن ۲ – Computer (High-level Architecture – CQRS)  
#### عنوان  
**CQRS: Separating Reads and Writes in Complex Systems**

#### متن  

CQRS, which stands for Command Query Responsibility Segregation, is an architectural pattern that separates read operations from write operations. Instead of using a single model to handle everything, a system with CQRS uses one model for commands that change state and another model for queries that read data. This separation can make complex domains easier to reason about and optimize.  

In large systems, CQRS is often combined with event sourcing or messaging. For example, when a command is handled, it may produce events that are stored and then used to update read models. This allows the write side to focus on business rules, while the read side is tuned for fast, efficient queries. However, CQRS also adds complexity, so it is usually recommended for systems where requirements are evolving and performance or scalability are critical.

## متن ۲ – Computer (Architecture – Observability)  
### عنوان  
**Observability: Understanding What Your System Is Doing**

### متن  

Observability is the ability to understand the internal state of a system by looking at its outputs, such as logs, metrics, and traces. In modern distributed systems, where many services run across multiple machines, observability is essential for detecting problems, finding bottlenecks, and understanding user behavior. Instead of guessing what went wrong, engineers rely on telemetry data that shows how requests move through the system.  

A typical observability stack includes structured logging for detailed events, metrics for numeric trends, and tracing for following a single request across services. Tools and platforms collect this data and present it in dashboards and alerts. This allows teams to spot anomalies quickly, correlate issues across different components, and reduce the time it takes to identify and fix incidents.

For example, a minimal API endpoint can receive a typed request object, call an injected service, and return results with proper status codes. This makes it possible to build clean, testable endpoints with very little code.

## متن ۲ – Computer (Architecture – Event Sourcing)  
### عنوان  
**Event Sourcing: Storing the History, Not Just the State**

### متن  

Event sourcing is an architectural pattern in which changes to application state are stored as a sequence of events instead of overwriting data. Each event describes what happened, such as “OrderPlaced” or “PaymentConfirmed,” and the current state can be rebuilt by replaying these events in order. This approach creates a complete audit log, which is useful for debugging, analytics, and compliance.  

In distributed systems, event sourcing is often combined with messaging and CQRS. When a command is handled, the system produces one or more events that are appended to an event store. Read models subscribe to these events and update their own data for fast queries. Although event sourcing adds complexity, it can make complex domains more transparent and easier to reason about over time.

## متن ۲ – Computer (Architecture – Distributed Transactions / Outbox)  
### عنوان  
**The Outbox Pattern: Reliable Messages in Distributed Systems**

### متن  

In distributed systems, writing to a database and sending a message at the same time can create consistency problems if one operation succeeds and the other fails. The outbox pattern solves this by first storing outgoing messages in the same database transaction as the business data. After the transaction commits, a separate process reads the outbox table and publishes the messages to a message broker.  

This approach ensures that either both the data change and the message are saved, or neither is, which reduces the risk of lost or duplicated events. It also works well with event-driven architectures and can be combined with patterns like event sourcing and CQRS. Although the outbox pattern adds operational complexity, it provides a practical way to achieve reliable messaging without using distributed transactions.

## متن ۲ – Computer (Architecture – Orders Domain + CQRS/Event Sourcing)  
### عنوان  
**Order Service with CQRS and Events**

### متن  

In a modern e‑commerce system, an Order service often uses CQRS to separate commands that change orders from queries that read them. Commands such as “PlaceOrder” or “CancelOrder” validate business rules and then produce events like “OrderPlaced” or “OrderCancelled.” These events are stored and published so that other services, such as Payment or Inventory, can react without tight coupling.  

On the read side, the system builds one or more read models that are optimized for queries, for example a list of recent orders for a customer or a dashboard for admins. When new order events arrive, these read models are updated asynchronously. This combination of CQRS and events makes it easier to add new features, audit changes over time, and scale reads and writes independently as traffic grows.

## متن ۲ – Computer (Architecture – Payment Service & Saga)  
### عنوان  
**Payment Service and Sagas in a Distributed Order System**

### متن  

In an e‑commerce system with separate Order, Payment, and Inventory services, a payment process often spans multiple steps and services. Instead of using a single distributed transaction, many teams use the saga pattern to manage a long‑running workflow. A saga is a sequence of local transactions, each followed by an event; if something fails along the way, compensating actions are triggered to undo or fix previous steps.  

For example, when an order is placed, the Order service publishes an “OrderPlaced” event. The Payment service reserves or captures funds and then emits “PaymentSucceeded” or “PaymentFailed.” If payment fails, the saga may cancel the order and release any reserved inventory. This approach keeps each service independent, while still coordinating the overall business process through events and compensations.
